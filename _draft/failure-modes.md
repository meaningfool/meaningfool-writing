# It's All About Failure Modes

![It's all about failure modes](@images/failures-modes.jpg)

1. With AI-based products came the concept of "failure modes" (as opposed to bugs). It was a new word in my vocabulary (non-native speaker here). But I realized it was resonating with my experience as a Product Manager.

2. Software products fail mechanistically. Same causes, same consequences. A failure is a bug. When the bug is fixed, the failure is gone.

3. AI products fail non-deterministically. Same causes, different scenarios. A failure is a signal. "Look at the data" (@hamelhusain mantra) to collect signals. Signals form patterns: the failure modes.

4. As a PM, reasoning on failure modes feels familiar: at the user level, failure is not 0/1. Only through interviews and proper segmentation can you extract cohesive failure patterns.

5. At the market level, identifying unmet needs is about understanding how existing products fail to meet specific segments' constraints and expectations.

6. Complex-enough systems are non-deterministic. The methods built to reason within human systems turn out to be what reasonning about AI systems require.
